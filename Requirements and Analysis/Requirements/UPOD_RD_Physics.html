1. Introduction

1.1 Scope

UPOD (Undergraduate Physics Online Database) is a website designed for the students with information about physics. The goal is to provide accurate and up to date physics knowledge for undergraduate students. There are currently very few quality physics reference sites, so UPOD has the opportunity to fill a large gap facing physics students at present.

1.2 Definitions, Acronyms, and Abbreviations

Fundamentals

  • basic materials, properties and forces in our world. It covers all sections of physics from mechanics to atomic physics, and gives a general understanding as to what physic properties governs our world.

    • Classical Mechanics

  • physical laws that describe the motion of bodies under the influence of a system of forces.

    • Optics

  • the properties and behaviour of light in conjunction with matter and the various instruments that either use or detect it.

    • Electricity and Magnetism

  • physics that involves the electromagnetic force, which is an interaction that occurs between two electrically charged particles. Magnetism is the physical interaction that occurs as a result of a motion of electric charge that is usually a result of attractive and repulsive forces between objects.

    • Quantum Mechanics

  • Quantum Mechanics is the specific branch of physics that deals with the very miniscule objects. It deals with the mathematical description of the motion and interaction of subatomic particles, which incorporates wave-particle duality, uncertainty principle, and concepts of quantization.

    • Statistical Mechanics

  • Statistical Mechanics is the study of the statistics of the behaviour of very large number of atoms or molecules. It takes into account the distribution of energy among the large number of molecules.

    2. Overall Description

    2.1 Product Perspective

    Integrating physics research and information into the new UPOD website, will be more interactive and user friendly as well as containing more relevant information than current websites with similar functions. UPOD's implementation of the equations and other features will be done in collaboration with the MathJax, Animations, and Front-end team, as the functionality and availability of the physics information will only be made available and interactive through these other teams.

    2.1.1 System Interfaces

  • operates on the modern and standard browsers, including Google Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Internet Explorer and Microsoft Edge.

    • 2.1.2 User Interfaces

    The Front-end team is responsible for the overall design and functionality of the UPOD website. This involves UI/UX implementation and developing guidelines for other teams to follow when designing assets for the website. UPOD’s MathJax team creates the physics equations as assets to be implemented into the website that are shown to students. The Graphics and Animations teams is responsible for developing the diagrams, and animated assets of the website. They follow any guidelines given by the Front-end team and match the physics requirements set out by the Physics team.

    2.1.3 Software Interfaces

    A database is used by the entire UPOD team to store and organize data. In the database, relevant information is linked together and able to include various information, equations, as well as any interactive diagrams that can be used.

    • Include various equations regarding the certain aspect of physics
    • Ensure that information is not redundant and stored only once
    • Include information and brief descriptions involving the aspect of physics
    • Improve the linkage between any physics information so that they can be used in unison with one another

    2.1.4 Communications Interfaces

    Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP)

  • HTTP is the underlying protocol used by the World Wide Web, which is a set of rules for transferring files.

    • 2.1.5 Memory

  • Is Memory relevant to physics information (the only problem would be exactly how to store all the phyiscs information and how much can be stored in linkage with ones that are related).
  • Most of the physics information is stored on Github for the time being which serves as a central database that contains all the data relevant to the six categories of physics covered by UPOD.

    • 2.2 Constraints

  • The budget of UPOD is zero, so any software must be free to use.

    • 3. Specific Requirements

    3.1 External Interfaces

  • The physics information and research that is provided by the Physics Team is be used in conjunction with the MathJax and Animations Team to provide creative ways to help the user learn about the physics concepts.
  • The MathJax Team uses the physics information collected to showcase sample problems and show equations that the user can see and interact with
  • The Animations Team uses the physics information to provide the UPOD website with interactive diagrams and animations for the user to better understand the material.

    • 3.2 Functions

  • The physics information that includes equations and formulas as well as appropriate
    information pertaining to the physics topic being covered must be correct.
  • The information is used by the MathJax and Animations Team to display certain
    interactive diagrams and sample problems/links to videos that will help the user better
    understand the topic at hand.
  • The equations that is displayed by the MathJax Team must allow users to rearrange
    equations and formulas and use them in sample problems that are given.
  • The units and explanations for each variable for each equation must be displayed and remain
    visible as to show the user exactly what each formula is related to and how it is useful in
    the topic they are looking at.
  • The animations and videos that are linked to the physics topic covered must be relevant to the
    physics topic that it is covering.
  • Links to sample problems must also be visible under 'Suggestions' as to show possible additional help that the user can receive.

    • 3.3 Performance Requirements

  • The physics research and information collected from the Physics Team is used to as a basis for the whole UPOD website to function around.
  • Everything from the information projected to the libraries of information available on the UPOD website is supported by the physics information collected.
  • The equations and formulas produced by MathJax is focused on mainly presenting the equations and formulas of the physics concepts and topics that is covered by UPOD.
  • The Animations and the overall look of the UPOD website is derived from physics information and reflects the whole aspect of the six categories of physics that UPOD is currently covering.

    • 3.4 Logical Database Requirements

    Types of information used by various functions

  • Physics team makes sure that all relevant physics information regarding the six categories of physics covered in the UPOD website is available
  • The information is used by the MathJax, Animations, and Front End team at all stages of their implementation
  • The physics information includes interactive diagrams when necessary

    • Frequency of use

  • The information is used at all stages of the UPOD project

    • Data entities and their relationships

  • It is crucial that any information regarding one aspect of physics that can be correlated to another aspect of physics can be presented in a way that can link them together
  • Any information that is relevant to another part of a physic information is important to include to show any similarities or comparisons

    • Types of Information Used by Various Functions

  • Global variables like constants are important to include (must be able to include all constant variables used) - e.g. Force of gravity (gravity = 9.81 m /s^2) or (e = mc^2)

    • 3.5 Software System Attributes

    3.5.1 Reliability

  • Database involving physics information must be up to date and include accurate and relevant information to the concept at hand
  • No duplicate information is stored
  • The equations regarding the physics information must be correct and relevant to the topic
  • Any physics concepts explained must be correct and must be concise, easy to read, and easy to understand by any user

    • 3.5.2 Availability

  • Physics information must be available at all points of the UPOD system in order to ensure that it is easy to take information regarding the physics branch and implement it into the system.

    • 3.5.3 Security

  • Without appropriate permission and consent from the SQA team, the information regarding the physics information cannot be updated, edited, removed, or added.

    • 3.5.4 Maintainability

  • any information that is to be added, updated, removed, or edited in the future regarding physics must be documented in the future
  • documentation and making sure that information are in correct components of the database ensure that the UPOD website is easy to maintain and can be easily changed in the future.
